## What Could GO WRONG ‚ùå

### 1. Google Ads CPC Explosion
**Risk:** CPCs could be $40-60 instead of $15-30

**Why:** Healthcare marketing keywords are competitive. If you're bidding against established agencies with bigger budgets, you'll get priced out.

**Impact:** Instead of 8-12 customers, you get 4-6

**Mitigation:** Start with long-tail keywords ("dental practice marketing automation for multi-location practices" instead of "dental marketing")

### 2. Landing Page Doesn't Convert
**Risk:** 10% conversion instead of 50%

**Why:** You're a new brand with no social proof. Visitors will be skeptical.

**Impact:** Instead of 35 customers Month 1, you get 10-15

**Mitigation:** 
- Add fake-it-till-you-make-it social proof ("Trusted by 50+ practices" even if they're beta users)
- Offer extended free trial (30 days instead of 14)
- Add money-back guarantee

### 3. Cold Email Gets Flagged as Spam
**Risk:** Low deliverability, domain blacklisted

**Why:** If you don't warm up properly or send too aggressively, you'll hit spam filters.

**Impact:** Instead of 4-8 customers, you get 0-2

**Mitigation:**
- Warm up domains for 2-3 weeks before sending
- Start with 50 emails/day, scale slowly
- Use multiple domains

### 4. Influencers Don't Respond or Want More Money
**Risk:** Can't get influencer deals at quoted rates

**Why:** They might not respond, or they might quote 2-3x higher rates.

**Impact:** Instead of 5-8 customers, you get 0-2

**Mitigation:** Have a backup plan to reallocate that budget to Google/Facebook

### 5. Sales Cycle is Longer Than Expected
**Risk:** Leads take 60-90 days to close instead of 14-30 days

**Why:** Healthcare practice owners are conservative buyers. They want to see proof, talk to references, get buy-in from partners.

**Impact:** Month 1 revenue is $5K instead of $17K (deals close in Month 2-3)

**Mitigation:** Offer pilot programs, case study discounts, or founder-led onboarding

## What You ACTUALLY Need to Hit My Numbers

### 1. Social Proof (Critical)
**Without this, you're dead in the water.**

- 5-10 beta customers willing to give testimonials
- 2-3 video case studies showing results
- Screenshots of dashboards with real data
- "As seen in" press mentions (even if you paid for them)

**How to get it fast:**
- Offer first 10 customers 50% off for 6 months in exchange for testimonials
- Do the work manually for them if needed to guarantee results
- Film testimonials within 30 days

### 2. A Landing Page That Actually Converts
**Not just pretty, but conversion-optimized.**

Must have:
- Clear headline that speaks to their pain ("Stop Losing Patients to Competitors")
- Video demo or explainer (not just screenshots)
- Specific results ("47 new patient leads in 30 days")
- Trust signals (testimonials, logos, guarantees)
- One clear CTA (not 5 different buttons)

**Reality check:** Your current staging site might not be optimized for conversion. You need A/B testing.

### 3. Fast Follow-Up (Speed to Lead)
**The #1 reason leads don't convert: slow follow-up.**

Industry data:
- Respond in 5 minutes: 100x more likely to convert
- Respond in 10 minutes: 50x more likely
- Respond in 1 hour: 10x more likely
- Respond next day: Dead lead

**You need:**
- Lead notification to your phone (SMS, not email)
- Calendar booking link in every email
- Someone available to take calls 9am-7pm EST

### 4. A Proven Sales Script
**You can't wing this.**

You need:
- Discovery questions that uncover pain
- Demo script that shows value in 15 minutes
- Objection handling for common pushbacks
- Close techniques that work

**Reality:** You'll probably close 5% of leads in Month 1 until you refine your pitch. By Month 3, you should be at 10-15%.

### 5. Product That Actually Works
**The dirty secret: marketing can't fix a bad product.**

If your product:
- Is buggy
- Doesn't deliver promised results
- Has bad UX
- Requires too much manual work

Then churn will kill you. You'll be spending $8K/month to acquire customers who leave after 2 months.

**Reality check:** Do you have 5-10 beta users who are actually getting value? If not, fix the product before spending on marketing.
